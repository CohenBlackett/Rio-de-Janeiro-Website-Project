About Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is a major Brazilian city known for its stunning natural landscape, iconic landmarks like the Christ the Redeemer statue and Sugarloaf Mountain, and vibrant culture, including its world-famous Carnival and samba music. Historically the capital of Brazil, it's a global hub for tourism, finance, and transport, situated on the country's Atlantic coast amidst dramatic mountains and Guanabara Bay. While it's a city of contrasts with both affluence and areas of concern like favelas, its cultural richness, beautiful beaches such as Copacabana and Ipanema, and significant role in major international events secure its place as one of the most significant cities in the Southern Hemisphere.

Fun facts:

    • Population: 6.2 million residents
      Home to a World Wonder: The iconic Christ the Redeemer statue, inaugurated in 1931, was voted one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007.
      Named for a Non-Existent River: When Portuguese explorers arrived in January 1502, they mistook Guanabara Bay for the mouth of a river, leading to the name "River of January" (Rio de Janeiro).
      Home to the World's Largest Urban Forest: The city's stunning natural landscape includes Tijuca Forest, the largest urban forest in the world, which contains a variety of flora and fauna.
