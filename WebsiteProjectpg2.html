About O Cristo Redentor

The Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor) statue is a large Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Perched atop the 700-meter-high Corcovado mountain, the statue has become a global symbol of Christianity, as well as a cultural icon for both Rio de Janeiro and all of Brazil.

Fun Facts:

    • The statue is frequently struck by lightning: Due to its position atop Corcovado mountain, it is hit several times a year during storms. A particularly intense storm in 2014 damaged the statue's thumb and head.
      There is a chapel at the base of the statue: The small Catholic chapel is dedicated to Our Lady of Aparecida, the patron saint of Brazil, and can be used for weddings and baptisms.
      The head and hands of the statue were sculpted in France: Though the monument was a collaboration of several designers, the Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa worked with the Polish-French sculptor Paul Landowski and the French engineer Albert Caquot to bring the statue to life.
      The statue was originally designed differently: An earlier version by engineer Heitor da Silva Costa depicted Christ carrying a large cross in one hand and a globe in the other. The final version, with Christ's arms spread wide, was chosen instead.
